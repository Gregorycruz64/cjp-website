About Us

Our History

Founded in 2024 by a coalition of human rights activists and economic reformers, the Citizens Justice Party of Kenya emerged from a need for a political movement truly centered on the people's welfare, not just rhetoric.

Our Vision

A Kenya where justice is accessible to the poorest citizen, where economic opportunities are equitable, and where the government serves as a diligent steward of public trust and resources.

Our Mission

To mobilize citizens towards active participation in governance, to implement radical transparency laws, and to build infrastructure that connects all 47 counties to the global economy.

Jacob Mantilli Kinganga

Party Chairman

Kenya stands at a defining moment in its history. For too long, politics has been reduced to promises, survival in power, and systems that serve a few while leaving millions struggling. Our people work hard, follow the rules, and still find themselves locked out of opportunity. This reality must change. Our party was founded on a simple but powerful belief: leadership must restore dignity, opportunity, and fairness to every citizen. Government should not exist to preserve political elites; it must exist to empower the people. True leadership does not create dependency—it creates the conditions for citizens to build their own prosperity. Across our country, millions of young people remain unemployed despite their talent and determination. Families struggle to educate their children. Communities lack the investment needed to unlock their potential. Meanwhile, corruption continues to drain resources that should be transforming lives. This is unacceptable. Our mission is clear. We will champion a new era of development that focuses on jobs, education, innovation, and accountable leadership. Instead of empty handouts, we will pursue sustainable economic programs that create real employment opportunities and equip our youth with modern skills—particularly in digital technology, entrepreneurship, and emerging global industries. We believe that every constituency and every community has the potential to become a center of growth. With transparent leadership, strategic investment, and strong partnerships—both locally and internationally—we can mobilize the resources necessary to transform our communities. Our vision includes modern educational institutions, technology-driven learning, and programs that prepare our young people for the future economy. But transformation requires courage. It requires leaders who are willing to speak the truth, challenge broken systems, and try new solutions. Progress has never come from playing it safe; it comes from bold ideas and persistent effort. History reminds us that nations advance when their leaders dare to experiment, innovate, and act decisively. We honor the legacy of those who fought for freedom and opportunity before us, but we must now build a future worthy of the next generation. Our commitment is simple: to serve the people with integrity, to fight corruption without compromise, and to pursue policies that restore hope and opportunity for every Kenyan. The journey ahead will not be easy. But if we work together—with courage, unity, and determination—we can renew the promise of our nation and build a future where every citizen has the tools to succeed. The time to act is now. The future belongs to those who are bold enough to build it.

2024-2028 Manifesto

A comprehensive plan for Kenya's transformation. We commit to these five pillars of development.

1

Economic Empowerment

Establishment of a sovereign wealth fund to ensure future generations benefit from current resources. Tax incentives for SMEs and mandatory local content quotas in government procurement to boost local manufacturing.

2

Universal Healthcare

Full implementation of the Social Health Authority (SHA) with zero-cost access to primary care. Construction of 50 new Level 4 hospitals in underserved counties and digitalization of all health records.

3

Education Reform

Integration of technology in all public schools by 2026. Free STEM education for girls in ASAL regions and a national apprenticeship program linking vocational training directly to industry needs.

4

Governance & Anti-Corruption

Mandatory lifestyle audits for all public officials. Digitization of all land registries to eliminate fraud. Whistleblower protection act with financial rewards for exposing graft.
